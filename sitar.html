Standard sitar

This is the most common type of sitar and is often used in classical Indian music. It has six or seven playable strings and 12-20 sympathetic strings.

Kharaj Pancham sitar

This type of sitar has a larger body and thicker strings than the standard sitar. It's often used in Indian classical music to play deep, bass notes.

Surbahar

Also known as the bass sitar, the Surbahar has a larger body and longer neck than the standard sitar.

₹ 15299/-

Vilayat Khan style sitar

This type of sitar is named after Ustad Vilayat Khan, a famous sitar player from India. It has a slightly smaller body and a thinner neck than the standard sitar.
